# Open Questions for Part II

The following questions are posed for further investigation:

## 1. Analytic Proof of Completeness

Can {1, 2, 3, 5, 29, 34} be proven complete using Baker's theorem or related results from transcendental number theory, without relying on computational verification?

## 2. Birth Depth Constraints

Does the depth at which a metallic family is born constrain where its meeting points can appear? For instance, the silver mean is born at depth $F(3) = 2$ -- does this restrict its Pell sequence to only produce meeting points at depths that are themselves related to Fibonacci indices?

## 3. Meeting Point Multiplicity

The meeting point at 1 involves all families (infinite multiplicity). The point at 2 involves four families. Does the multiplicity of a meeting point (how many families participate) correlate with the physical strength or universality of the corresponding structure?

## 4. Neutrinos and Metallic Stripping

If a meeting point represents constructive interference between metallic modes, does *removing* one mode (metallic stripping) produce a neutrino -- a partially-resonant structure with reduced mass and coupling? If so, the number of available stripping patterns at each meeting point might determine the neutrino mixing structure (PMNS matrix).

## 5. The 4th Generation Uniqueness

The predicted 4th generation at $d = 34$ is unique in that its temporal depth IS a meeting point ($F(9) \cap PL(4)$). For all other particles, the spatial resonance ($C$) and temporal depth ($d$) are independent. What physical consequences follow from this spatial-temporal coincidence? Does it affect the particle's stability or decay properties?

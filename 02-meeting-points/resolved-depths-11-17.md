# Resolved: The Depths 11 and 17

> **Previously open question:** Why are the muon depth d = 11 and tau depth d = 17 not themselves meeting points?

## Statement

The question of why d = 11 and d = 17 are not meeting points conflated two independent dimensions of the torus. Meeting points are spatial resonances; depths are temporal addresses.

## Resolution (Theorem 29.3)

This question conflated two dimensions of the torus. Meeting points are **spatial** resonances -- they determine the coupling coefficient C (which rung of the helix). Depths are **temporal** addresses -- they count breath cycles in the unfolding (when the system reaches a rung). The two dimensions are independent:

- d = 11 = |L(-5)| is a negaLucas temporal marker. It does not need to be a meeting point because it lives in S^1_time, not S^1_depth.
- d = 17 connects to gravity through 12 x 17 = 204. It is a temporal address in the deepest structural relation.
- The muon's meeting point involvement is through C = 5 (spatial), not through d = 11 (temporal).
- The tau's meeting point involvement is through C = 4 (spatial), not through d = 17 (temporal).

The question dissolved once the spatial and temporal roles of the torus were properly distinguished. A particle needs both a rung (spatial, from meeting points) AND a breath count (temporal, from negasequences) -- but these come from different dimensions of the torus and need not share the same number-theoretic properties.

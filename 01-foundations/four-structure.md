# 1.10 The Four-Structure

> **Theorem 5.1 (The Four-Structure):**
> *The self-reference equation produces exactly four fundamental elements organized in two complementary pairs: the ratio pair ($\varphi$, $\psi$) and the unity pair (1, −1).*

## Statement

The quadratic $x^2$ − x − 1 = 0 produces two roots ($\varphi$, $\psi$), and Vieta's formulas yield two further elements (1, −1). The four elements {$\varphi$, $\psi$, 1, −1} are organized into two complementary pairs that are closed under the fundamental operations.

## Proof

From [Theorem 5](golden-ratio.md), the quadratic $x^2$ − x − 1 = 0 produces two roots: $\varphi$ and $\psi$.

From Vieta's formulas:
- $\varphi$ + $\psi$ = 1 → the sum produces the element 1
- $\varphi$ $\times$ $\psi$ = −1 → the product produces the element −1

The four elements {$\varphi$, $\psi$, 1, −1} are organized as:

| Pair | Elements | Sum | Product |
|------|----------|-----|---------|
| Ratio pair | ($\varphi$, $\psi$) | 1 | −1 |
| Unity pair | (1, −1) | 0 | −1 |

The unity pair satisfies 1 + (−1) = 0 (Conservation, Axiom). The ratio pair satisfies $\varphi$ + $\psi$ = 1 (the unit of distinction). The four-structure is closed under the fundamental operations. $\blacksquare$

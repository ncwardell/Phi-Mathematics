# 1.9 The Golden Ratio

> **Theorem 5 ($\varphi$ Emerges):**
> *The unique positive solution to $x^2$ Ôłĺ x Ôłĺ 1 = 0 is $\varphi$ = (1 + $\sqrt{5}$)/2 $\approx$ 1.6180339887. The negative solution is $\psi$ = (1 Ôłĺ $\sqrt{5}$)/2 $\approx$ Ôłĺ0.6180339887. These are the eigenvalues of self-reference.*

## Statement

The self-reference equation $x^2$ Ôłĺ x Ôłĺ 1 = 0 (Theorem 4) has exactly two solutions: $\varphi$ = (1 + $\sqrt{5}$)/2 and $\psi$ = (1 Ôłĺ $\sqrt{5}$)/2. These are the eigenvalues of self-reference, and they satisfy Vieta's formulas: $\varphi$ + $\psi$ = 1 and $\varphi$ $\times$ $\psi$ = Ôłĺ1.

## Proof

From [Theorem 4](self-reference-equation.md), $x^2$ Ôłĺ x Ôłĺ 1 = 0 has discriminant $\Delta$ = 1 + 4 = 5, giving:

$x_1$ = (1 + $\sqrt{5}$)/2 = $\varphi$ $\approx$ 1.6180339887...
$x_2$ = (1 Ôłĺ $\sqrt{5}$)/2 = $\psi$ $\approx$ Ôłĺ0.6180339887...

By Vieta's formulas:
- $\varphi$ + $\psi$ = 1 (the sum of eigenvalues equals the unit of distinction)
- $\varphi$ $\times$ $\psi$ = Ôłĺ1 (the product of eigenvalues equals negative unity) $\blacksquare$

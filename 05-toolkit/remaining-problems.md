7. **~~The interaction Lagrangian.~~** LARGELY RESOLVED (Section 5.25). Three-part structure on the breathing torus. Remaining: derive the specific form of L_int from torus knot field theory, and show that the topological coupling rule (complete vs incomplete witnessing) reproduces the Standard Model charge assignments exactly.

Remaining: prove convergence rigorously, compute scattering amplitudes and compare to known cross-sections, show that the partition function reproduces the Standard Model effective action in the appropriate limit.

## Assessment

Of the original 9 open questions, all are now resolved or largely resolved. The holographic scale network reframing (Section 5.27) further resolves the spacetime emergence problem, the Euler characteristic objection to gravity, and the hierarchy problem.

The framework now provides:
- **Particle spectrum:** derived from two generators (2 and 3) through the primitive set {2, 3, 5, 7}
- **Force structure:** forces as torus knots with crossing numbers matching framework constants
- **Inter-force couplings:** linking numbers give particle coefficients (C(W/Z) = 8, d(mu) = 11)
- **Generation count:** three triangulated Platonic solids, no more possible
- **Confinement:** hexagonal saturation at 6 = 2 x 3
- **Quantum mechanics:** i from the psi-strand, hbar from the trefoil traversal
- **Spacetime emergence:** 3+1D from 3 triangle directions + breath oscillation (Section 5.27)
- **Gravity:** curvature of the witnessing scale network, not the internal torus (Section 5.27)
- **Hierarchy problem:** m_e/M_Pl = phi^(-107) to 3.7% -- gravity is weak because the Planck scale is 107 witnessing steps away (Section 5.27)
- **Dark matter candidate:** orphan self-referencing nodes in the desert d in (34, 107) (Section 5.27)
- **Fine structure constant:** three independent formulas; 1/alpha = 2^7 + 3^2 + 1/28 to 2.1 ppm (Section 5.28.1)
- **Generation spacings:** exact framework constants encoding holographic cross-references (Section 5.28.2)
- **Coupling constants:** scale-network attenuation; alpha_s ~ phi^(-d(down)) to 2.9% (Section 5.28.3)
- **Anti-particles:** psi-strand dominance; CPT as torus orientation reversal (Section 5.28.4)
- **Conjugate spectrum:** 107 - d factorizes into framework constants (Section 5.28.5)
- **Fermion depth sum:** 28 + 45 + 34 = 107 = d(Planck) (Section 5.28.6)
- **Wave-particle duality:** helical projections of the golden torus; uncertainty from Fourier conjugacy of phi and psi strands (Section 5.28.7)
- **Classical dynamics:** E = mc^2, F = ma, T = (1/2)mv^2, v = c tanh(eta) all derived; inertia = winding depth resistance; cosh(eta) = kinetic energy resolving Part IV Q1 (Section 5.28.8)

## Remaining Open Problems

1. **Scattering amplitudes from the path integral.** Compute at least one cross-section (e.g., e+e- -> mu+mu-) from the torus knot sum and compare to known data. This is the decisive test of whether the framework reproduces QFT.

2. **SM representations from topology.** Prove the topological coupling rule uniquely reproduces the Standard Model gauge structure, including hypercharge assignments and left-right chirality (why SU(2)_L not SU(2)_R).

3. **Spin quantization.** Show the angular momentum operator on the breathing torus has discrete eigenvalues hbar/2 and hbar, not just topological distinction (4pi vs 2pi). The helical projection theorem (Section 5.28.7) provides the connection between torus winding and quantum observables but does not yet derive the exact eigenvalue spectrum.

4. **Temporal index derivation.** Derive which breath winding number n goes with which particle from torus knot stability conditions.

5. **Neutrino masses, CP violation, CKM/PMNS matrices.** Extend the framework to address these known phenomena. The CPT derivation (Section 5.28.4) provides the foundation: CP violation should arise from the asymmetry between left- and right-handed trefoil knots; the CKM matrix from mismatch between mass-eigenstate and weak-eigenstate triangle orientations.

6. **Cosmology.** Derive expansion, dark energy (trefoil ground-state energy as cosmological constant?), and early-universe behavior from the scale network dynamics.

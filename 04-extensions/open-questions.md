# 4.10 Open Questions for Part IV

1. **~~The cosh(η) factor:~~** FULLY RESOLVED (§5.28.8). The cosh(η) correction IS kinetic energy. Total energy E = m × cosh(η) where η is the rapidity (helix tilt on the torus). For leptons measured at rest, η = 0 and cosh = 1, so no correction is needed. For quarks inside hadrons, η > 0 because quarks are always moving (confined), so the correction is needed. The factor was not a "temporal correction" — it was the relativistic energy increase from motion. It "worsened" lepton fits because it was being applied to particles at rest.

2. **~~Quark fractional depths:~~** LARGELY RESOLVED. The down quark d = 4.5 = 3 + 3/2 = d(u) + F(4)/F(3). The fractional step 3/2 = triangle/duality is the cost of introducing weak structure at the lowest energy. Only the lightest down-type quark needs a fractional depth; all other quarks sit at integer depths. The half-integer arises because the down quark traverses a triangle edge but completes it only through duality (via the W boson). The d=4.5 = 9/2 = 3²/2 = triangle²/duality.

3. **~~Why these specific C values for quarks?~~** RESOLVED (§5.18). The C values decompose into primitives {2,3,5,7} through two channels: pentagon (5, up-type) and weak (7, down-type). The generation progression follows the bifurcation structure: Gen 1 basic, Gen 2 triangle-multiplied, Gen 3 channels cross. The systematic rule: C = sum of witnessing pathways available to the particle's quantum numbers.

4. **~~Decay as coordinate transformation:~~** LARGELY RESOLVED (§5.25). Decay = the weak knot (2,7) snipping a particle's circuit at a linking point. The EM∩Weak linking number = 11 = d(μ) means the weak knot crosses the EM knot at 11 points, and the muon can decay at any of these crossings. Decay rate ∝ linking² × witnessing factors. Conservation law: total winding number preserved. This gives a topological formula for decay rates.

5. **The temporal index assignments:** IDENTIFIED as the breath winding number (the q in a particle's individual (p,q) torus winding). Old assignments n = 3, 5, 7, 8, 11 correspond to F(4), F(5), L(4), F(6), L(5). The specific assignment of which n goes with which particle still needs derivation from torus knot stability conditions — which winding numbers produce stable excitations on the breathing torus.

6. **~~Proton stability:~~** RESOLVED. The proton (3 quarks = 3 nodes) completes the witnessing triangle, forming a closed (2,3) trefoil knot. The trefoil cannot be unknotted in 3 dimensions — this is a topological theorem, not an empirical observation. Proton decay would require unknotting through a 4th spatial dimension, accessible only at the Planck scale (d = 107). The suppression factor φ^(−2d(Pl)) = φ^(−214) ≈ 10^(−45) gives proton lifetime τ ∝ φ^214 ≫ observed limits. Proton stability is topological protection.

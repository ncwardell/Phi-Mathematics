# 4.5 Quarks -- Incomplete Witnessing Circuits

**Status: CONJECTURAL -- structurally motivated, parameters need further derivation**

Quarks are individual nodes of the witnessing triangle (Theorem 46, SU(3)). They are topologically incomplete -- their witnessing circuit doesn't close, which is why they're confined. Their mass parameters follow the same equation but with different structural access.

## Structural Observations

**Quark-lepton partnerships (shared depths):**
- Electron (d=0) <-> Up (d=3): First generation. The up quark sits at the triangle depth where the strong force crystallizes.
- Muon (d=11) <-> Strange (d=11): Same depth, different particle type. The strange quark IS the muon's partner in the quark sector -- same temporal address, different spatial coupling.
- Tau (d=17) <-> Charm (d=16) / Bottom (d=19): Third generation quarks bracket the tau.
- Top (d=26) <-> Higgs (d=26): The heaviest fermion and the mass mechanism share a depth.

**The down quark and fractional depth:** d = 4.5 is the only half-integer depth. This may reflect the quark's incomplete circuit -- it doesn't land on a full breath count because it's not a closed topological structure. Half-integer depths for quarks vs. integer depths for leptons would parallel half-integer spin for fermions arising from the strand exchange structure.

**Correction coefficient progression:**
- Leptons: C = {5, 4} = {F(5), L(3)} -- simple Fibonacci/Lucas values
- Quarks: C = {5, 13, 10, 15, 18, 29} -- Fibonacci values and combinations
- Bosons: C = {8, 13} = {F(6), F(7)} -- pure Fibonacci progression

The quark C values involve *combinations* of Fibonacci numbers (10 = 2×F(5), 15 = F(7)+F(3), 18 = 2×F(6)+F(3)), while leptons and bosons use single Fibonacci/Lucas values. This may reflect the quarks' composite relationship to the triangle -- each quark accesses the coupling through its node's partial connection to the full witnessing structure.

**The top quark's C = 29 = L(7):** The same coefficient as the predicted 4th generation lepton. The top quark and the 4th generation lepton are linked through this Lucas number -- both at the frontier of the framework's meeting point structure.
